6. Why kakanin is called kakanin? ​

The name kakanin is derived from two Tagalog words: “kain” (to eat) and “kanin” (rice).

Explanation:

It's an umbrella term for sweets made of glutinous rice and coconut milk, two ingredients that tropical countries like ours have in abundance. These ingredients are usually employed in one of two forms.

20. ang mga kinagigilingan kakanintulad ng halayang ube,inutak at panara ay matatagpuan sa ?


Answer:

Pateros and San Juan City

Explanation:

In these places, delicacies like bibingka, ube halaya, inutak and panara are often found in Pateros and San Juan  City. It's a delicious, must-try crossover between creamy ube jam, chewy bilo-bilo, and sticky sapin-sapin – rich, sweet ube halaya is topped with glutinous rice, coconut milk and coconut cream, baked together until gooey, chewy, and creamy. While there is no known history as to when the Filipinos started transforming ube into ube jalea, it has been part of the childhood of most Filipinos – being served at town fiestas, birthdays, during Christmas, and other celebrations. Inutak is a type of layered rice cake similar to sapin sapin, consisting of two flavours first is purple yam on the base then topped by a coconut cream flavoured topping. It is first cooked on stove top then baked or broiled to complete the cooking process at the same time give that spotted brown topping.

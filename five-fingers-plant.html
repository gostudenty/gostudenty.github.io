5. Humans and apes have five fingers they can use to grasp objects. This is an example of?


Answer:

Homologous structures

Human and apes have 5 fingers that are used for grasping objects, so they have the same function, but they also have a common ancestor and are closely related, that means that this is an example of homologous structures.

Answer:

Analogous structures are structures with different evolutionary ancestries but they have the same function. Examples include; wings of birds, insects and bats. On the other hand homologous structures have similar ancestries and common traits but may not have the same function in an organism. 9. animal cell and plant cell is a cell that are used to keep our fingers ​


Explanation:

Animal cells and plant cells are not used to keep our fingers. Animal and plant cells are the basic unit of life in animals and plants, respectively. They are composed of a cell membrane, cytoplasm, and a nucleus, and perform a variety of functions that are essential for the survival and growth of the organism. The cells in our fingers are called skin cells, and they play a crucial role in protecting our body from the external environment and maintaining the integrity of our skin.

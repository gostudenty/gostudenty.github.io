14. Learning Task No. 4 1. Myla bought a jar shaped like a rectangular prism having a length of 130 cm, a width of 70 cm and a height of 110 cm. How much water is needed to fill the jar? 2. To relieve her stress from work, Tina would always watch the fishes move around her 90 cm wide, 70 cm high, and 45 cm long aquarium. If the aquarium is filled halfway through its height, how much volume of water is in it? 3. A truck has container with dimensions 9 feet long, 7 feet wide, and 5 feet high is delivering sand for a sand sculpture competition. How many trips will it take for the truck to deliver 945 cubic feet of sand? pasagot ng maayos please


Answer :

1.

To find the volume of a rectangular prism, multiply its 3 dimensions: length x width x height. The volume is expressed in cubic units. The formula for the volume of a rectangular prism is, Volume (V) = base area × height of the prism. Another way to express this formula is, Volume = l × w × h.

Given:

130cm length, 70cm width and 110 cm height

Solution:

V = L × W × HV = 130 × 70 × 110V = 1,001,000 cm^3

Hence it needs to fill the jar with 1,001,000 cubic centimeters volume of Water.

2.

The problem also in no. 2 is a rectangular prism, hence the formula for finding the volume of a rectangular prism is V = L × W × H

Given:

90 cm length, 70cm height and 40cm width

Solution:

V = L × W × HV = 90 × 70 × 40V = 252,000 cm^3

Hence the volume of water inside the Aquarium is 252,000 cubic centimeters.

3.

Formula of finding the volume of a Rectangular Prism

V = L × W × H

Given:

9 ft, 7 ft, 5 ft

Solution:

V = L × W × HV = 9 × 7 × 5V = 315

Hence the Volume of the Truck is 315 cubic feet.

But, the question is : How many trips will it take for the truck to deliver 945 cubic feet of sand?

But a Truck Container is only 315 ft^3.

So The truck needs 3 TRIPS to deliver 945 cubic feet of sand. Back to Back deliver.

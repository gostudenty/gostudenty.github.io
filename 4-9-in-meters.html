7. The height of a square pyramid is 9 meters. What is the volume of the pyramid if a side of the base is 4 meters?


To find the volume of a square pyramid, we can use the formula V = (1/3)Bh, where B is the area of the base and h is the height of the pyramid.

In this case, the base of the pyramid is a square with sides of 4 meters, so the area of the base is B = 4m * 4m = 16m^2. The height of the pyramid is 9 meters, so we can plug these values into the formula to find the volume:

V = (1/3) * 16m^2 * 9m = 48m^3

Therefore, the volume of the square pyramid is 48 cubic meters.

21. a cylindrical water tank has a raduis of 4 meters and hieght of 9 meters. what is the volume of the water tank?​


Answer:

ANSWER

452.16 m³

FORMULA:

V = πr²h

MEANINGS:

V means volume

π means pi or 3.14 (rounded)

r means radius

² means squared

h means height

SOLUTION:

V = πr²h

V = (3.14)(4 m)²(9 m)

V = (3.14)(16 m²)(9 m)

V = (3.14)(144 m³)

V = 452.16 m³

18. You walk into your local grocery store and see two packages of ground beef that are identical aside from their labeling. One is labeled “80% lean,” and the other is labeled “20% fat.” Which package are you more likely to buy, and what behavioral economic concept does this example illustrate?


Answer:

In this scenario, it's likely that most people would choose the package labeled "80% lean" over the one labeled "20% fat." This decision is influenced by the behavioral economic concept of framing, which refers to the way in which information is presented to an individual and how it can affect their decision-making.

When presented with two options that are otherwise identical, people tend to prefer the one that is framed in a more positive or desirable way. In this case, "80% lean" is framed as a positive attribute, while "20% fat" is framed as a negative attribute. The framing of the information can change the individual's perception of the product, leading to a preference for the "80% lean" package over the "20% fat" package.

This example illustrates how the way information is presented can have a big impact on an individual's decision-making process. It's important to take into account how framing can affect the perceived value of a product and how it can influence consumer behavior.

Answer:

In this scenario, I would be more likely to buy the package of ground beef labeled "80% lean." This is because the labeling of the beef as "80% lean" highlights a desirable attribute of the product (a lower percentage of fat), while the labeling of the beef as "20% fat" highlights an undesirable attribute of the product (a higher percentage of fat).

This example illustrates the concept of "framing" in behavioral economics. Framing refers to the way in which information is presented, and it can influence the decisions that people make. In this case, the framing of the ground beef as "80% lean" versus "20% fat" can influence the decision of a consumer. The "80% lean" framing is more desirable and thus more likely to be chosen by the consumer, compared to the "20% fat" framing.

Additionally, this example also illustrates the concept of "anchoring" in behavioral economics. Anchoring refers to the way in which people's judgments and decisions are influenced by a given reference point. In this case, the "80%" and "20%" percentages act as anchors that influence the consumer's decision-making process. The "80%" figure is a higher percentage, which implies the ground beef is healthier, whereas the "20%" figure implies the opposite.

In summary, this example illustrates the concept of framing and anchoring in behavioral economics, highlighting how the way information is presented can influence consumer decisions.

It is likely that a person will choose the package labeled "80% lean" over the one labeled "20% fat."

This example illustrates the concept of "framing effect" in behavioral economics. Framing effect refers to the way in which the way information is presented (framed) can influence the choices that people make. In this case, the package labeled "80% lean" is framed in a positive light, making it more appealing to the consumer, while the package labeled "20% fat" is framed in a negative light, making it less appealing. This concept demonstrates how small changes in the way information is presented can have a significant impact on consumer behavior.

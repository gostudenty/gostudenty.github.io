26. What change was made in the second sentence? The holidays are just around the corner. The holidays are just around the corner, so I need to go shopping.


The difference between the two is that the first only has one Independent clause. This sentence is called a simple sentence. It is when a sentence is only composed of an independent clause.

In the other one, a coordinating conjunction (for, and, nor, but, or, yet, so) "so" was used and another independent clause was added. This sentence is called a complex sentence. It is when two or more independent clauses are joined together with a coordinating conjunction.

30. going ve the meaning idioms of cut any corners​


Answer:

going ve the meaning idioms of cut any corners

undertake something in what appears to be the easiest, quickest, or cheapest way, often by omitting to do something important or ignoring rules. This phrase comes from cutting (off) the corner , which means 'taking the shortest course by going across and not round a corner'.

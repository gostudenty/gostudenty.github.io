Step-by-step explanation:

A cylinder is a solid bounded by a closed cylindrical surface and two parallel planes.  

To calculate the volume of the cylinder, the following formula will be used  

if radius is given, use [tex]V = (\pi r^2)h[/tex] (Eqn 1)

if diameter is given, use  [tex]V = (\frac{\pi d^2}{4})h[/tex] (Eqn 2)

For numbers 1, 4,6,7, and 9, use Eqn 2.

For numbers 2, 3,5,and 8 use Eqn 1.

I will just show sample calculations using both equations and you can follow these solutions for other numbers afterwards.

Number 1. d = 8 m, h = 12 m

Using Eqn 2  we have

[tex]V = (\frac{\pi d^2}{4})h\\V = (\frac{\pi (8m)^2}{4})(12m)\\V = 603.19m^3[/tex]


Number 2. r = 9 in., h = 12 in

Using Eqn 1 we have

[tex]V = (\pi r^2)h\\V = (\pi (9in)^2)(12in)\\V = 3053.63in^3[/tex]

Therefore, the formula for calculating the volume of cylinder depends on what is given.

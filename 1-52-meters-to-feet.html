We will be using Law of Cosines,

[tex]c^{2} = a^{2} + b^{2} -2ab cos C[/tex].

Let's convert first 52°10.2' into decimal degree.

[tex]10.2' = 10.2' * \frac{1degree}{60'}\\\\= 0.17degrees[/tex]

=> 52°10.2' = 52°+10.2'

                  = 52°+0.17°

                  = 52.17°

Consider the diagram (see first photo below)

Let one diagonal, say AC, be 3325 feet and one side, say BC, be 2995 feet.

As the diagonals bisect each other {being a parallelogram}

So EC=1662.5 feet

    BC=2995 feet

Using Law of Cosines, we get,

[tex](BC)^{2} = (EB)^{2} + (EC)^{2} - 2(EB)(EC) cos (52.17degrees)\\=> 8970025 = (EB)^{2} + 2763906.25 - (EB)*3325*0.6133 \\=> EB^{2} - 2039.2913(EB)-6206118.75=0\\=> EB=3711.45, -1672.16[/tex]

AS EB is length so EB cannot be negative

Thus EB = 3711.45 feet

Also, BD = 2EB

               =  7422.9 feet  

The size of the other diagonal is 7422.9 feet

23. The length of a rectangular room is 2 feet longer than three times the width of the room. the perimeter of the room is 52 feet. write an equation and determine the length and width of the room.


Given:

Width = x feet
Length = 3x + 2 feet
Perimeter = 52 feet

Solution:

P = 2L + 2W
52 = 2 (3x + 2) + 2 (x)         ⇒ Equation
6x + 4 + 2x = 52
8x + 4 = 52
8x = 48
x = 6

Substitute:

W ⇒ x feet = 6 feet
L ⇒ 3x + 2 feet ⇒ 3(6) + 2 feet = 20 feet

Answer: The equation's 2 (3x + 2) + 2 (x) = 52 and the dimension is 6 feet by 20 feet.

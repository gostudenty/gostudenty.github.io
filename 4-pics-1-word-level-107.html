27. Dioxin is considered to be poisonous in concentrations above 2 ppb. If the lake containing 1 x 107 L has been contaminated by 0.1 g of dioxin, what is the concentration in ppb and did the concentration reach the dangerous level? a. 0.1 ppb, no it didn’t reach the dangerous level b. 10 ppb, yes it reached the dangerous level c. 0.01 ppb, no it didn’t reach the dangerous level d. 100 ppb, yes it reached the dangerous level


c. 0.01 ppb, no it didn’t reach the dangerous level

Further explanation

The concentration of a substance can be expressed in several quantities such as moles, percent (%) weight/volume,), molarity, molality, parts per million (ppm) or mole fraction. The concentration shows the amount of solute in a unit of the amount of solvent.

Solution = solvent + solute

ppb = parts per billion = one part per 10⁹ parts = one part of solute per one billion parts of solvent

Can be expressed in micrograms of substance per liter of water (μg / l)

0.1 g Dioxin = 10⁵ μg

so the concentration in ppb :

= 10⁵ μg : 1 x 10⁷ L

= 10⁻² μg / l

= 0.01 ppb

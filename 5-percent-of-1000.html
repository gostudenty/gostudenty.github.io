Problem

what is the sample size for a survey that will be taken randomly from a population of 1000 with 5 percent margin of error

Given

N = 1,000.00e = 5%n = ?

[tex]\bold{Formula\:||\:Sample_{Size} = \frac{N}{1\:+\: {N}^{2} } }[/tex][tex]\large\tt{\red{Sample_{Size}} = \frac{1,000}{1 \:+\: (1,000){(0.05)}^{2} } }[/tex]

Substitute the values of n and e to the formula and solve for the product at the denominator then simplify

[tex]\large\tt{Sample_{Size} = \frac{N}{1\: + \:{N}^{2} }}[/tex]

[tex]\large\tt{Sample_{Size} = \frac{1,000}{1 \:+ \:(1,000) (0.05)²}}[/tex]

[tex]\large\tt{Sample_{Size} = \frac{1,000}{ 1 \:+ \:(1,000) (0.0025) }}[/tex]

[tex]\large\tt{Sample_{Size} = \frac{1,000}{3.5}}[/tex]

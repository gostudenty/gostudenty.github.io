ProblemHow many said "It's more filling!" but didn't say “It tastes great!"?How many said neither of those things?

GivenTotal 110 dogs89 said "It tastes great!"87 said "It's more filling!"68 said "It tastes great!" and "It's more filling!"

Solution

Let

x stands for dogs who only said "It tastes great!"

y stands for dogs who only said "It's more filling!"

z stands for dogs who said neither of those things

x + y + z= 110

Remember, 89 said "It tastes great!" and 87 said "It's more filling!" those dogs also can either they said both.

Equation will be

89 - 68 = x, x = 21 dogs

87 - 68 = y, y = 19 dogs

Therefore, there are 19 dogs who said "It's more filling!" but didn't say “It tastes great!".

Solve for z

z = 110 - (x + y + 68)

z = 110 - (21 + 19 + 68)

z = 110 - 108

z = 2 dogs

Therefore, there are 2 dogs who said neither of those things.

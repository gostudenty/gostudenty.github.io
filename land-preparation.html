Answer:

1. After the last harvest or during the fallow period, the initial land preparation occurs. This is necessary for good weed control and soil enrichment. Preparing your land for planting takes about 34 weeks on average. It's critical that we don't conflate land clearing with land preparation.

2.Plowing is used to "till" or dig up, mix, and overturn the soil; harrowing is used to break up the soil clods and incorporate plant debris; and leveling is used to level the field. After your last harvest or throughout the fallow period, commence initial land preparation. This is necessary for good weed control and soil enrichment.

3.From zero-tillage or minimum tillage, which minimizes soil disturbance, to completely 'puddled' soil, which actually damages soil structure, land preparation encompasses a wide range of methods. Plowing is usually used to "till" or dig up, mix, and overturn the soil.

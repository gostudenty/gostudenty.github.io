Question:

A regular hexagon is inscribed in a circle what is the area of the circle if the side length of the regular hexagon is 6 cm? (Answer is rounded off to the nearest whole number.)

A. 103cm²

B. 113 cm²

C. 104cm²

D. 114 cm²​

Answer:

B. 113 cm²

SOLUTION:

A regular hexagon form 6 equilateral triangles. When a regular hexagon is inscribed in a circle, the length of its side is equal to the radius of the circle.

See attached picture for visual representation of the figure.

[tex]\large\overbrace{\underbrace{\text{$>>>$ see attached picture $<<<$}}}[/tex]

Hence radius of circle = side length of hexagon = 6 cm

Now solve for Area of Circle, using r = 6 cm

A = π r²

A = 3.14 (6)²

A = 3.14 (36)A = 113.04 cm² or round off to 113 cm²

[tex]\therefore \large\boxed{\text{\sf The Area of the circle is 113 $\sf cm^2$}}[/tex]

10. The rainfall in June was 11.2cm. This was 1cm less than twice the rainfall in July. Find the amount of rainfall in cm for July(with checking)


Answer:

Hi Joshua5657,


The answer to your problem is  

The amount of rainfall in July is 6.1 cm


Checking:

[tex]11.2 = 2x -1[/tex]

[tex]11.2 = 2(6.1) -1[/tex]

[tex]11.2 = 12.2 -1[/tex]

[tex]11.2 = 11.2[/tex]


Step-by-step explanation:

Here’s how we got this answer.

First we need to set the variables and set the equation such that


y = rainfall in June


x = rainfall in July


[tex]2x-1[/tex] = 1cm less than twice the rainfall in July


Hence our equation will be


[tex]y=2x-1[/tex]

But since we already know the amount of rainfall in June we can rephrase the equation to

[tex]11.2 = 2x - 1[/tex]

Now that we have established the variables we can now proceed to the next step which is to solve the problem.


We can eliminate the integer -1 from the right side of the equation by adding 1 on both sides.


[tex]11.2 + 1= 2x -1+1[/tex]

[tex]12.2 = 2x[/tex]

Dividing both sides by 2 will remove the integer 2 to the unknown variable thus giving us the result that we are looking for


[tex]\frac{12.2}{2} = \frac{2x}{2}[/tex]

[tex]x = \frac{12.2}{2}[/tex]

[tex]x = 6.1[/tex]

Hence the amount of rainfall in July is 6.1 cm.

18. Ano ang kahulugan Ng red rainfall advisory​


Answer:

Itinataas Red Rainfall Advisory kapag maituturing nang emergency ang pagbuhos ng ulan o malala ang magiging posibleng epekto nito. Kung mahigit 30 mm ang ulan sa susunod na isang oras, o kung tatlong oras nang malakas ang ulan at umabot na sa 65 mm, ang Red ay ipapalabas ng PAG-ASA.

AnswerAng red rainfall advisory ay ibinibigay kapag ang pagbuhos ng ulan ay bumubuo ng isang emergency. Tinaasan ito kapag ang naobserbahang pag-ulan ay higit sa 30 mm sa loob ng isang oras o kung ang ulan ay nagpatuloy sa nakaraang tatlong oras at higit sa 65 mm.


19. 2. What do PAGASA's yellow, orange, and red rainfall advarios mean?​


Under the heavy rainfall warning system, a yellow rainfall advisory is raised when the expected rainfall amount is between 7.5 mm and 15 mm within one hour and likely to continue.

Communities given this advisory are advised to be aware of the weather condition and warned that flooding may be possible in low-lying areas.

The orange rainfall advisory is raised in areas where rainfall is between 15 mm and 30 mm within one hour. Flooding is a definite threat in communities under the orange alert.

A red rainfall advisory is issued when downpours constitute an emergency. This is raised when observed rainfall is more than 30 mm within one hour or if rainfall has continued for the past three hours and is more than 65 mm.

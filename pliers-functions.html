6. What are the functions of the linsman's pliers​


Answer:

- Lineman's pliers are used in the electrical trade to cut, straighten, and bend wire, and also to twist wires together when making splices.

Function Of Lineman's Pliers

Lineman's Pliers or commonly known as Kleins in US and Canada is a hand tool that are used in the electrical trade to cut, straighten, and bend wire, and also to twist wires together when making splices.

Lineman’s pliers are distinguishable from other pliers by their stub nose, flat gripping surfaces, large cutting edges, and their angular edges on the outside of the jaw. Lineman’s pliers are primarily used by electricians. They came into existence with the birth of the telegraph industry in the mid 1800s, hence the name “Lineman’s Pliers.”

Lineman’s pliers are used by electricians to straighten, cut, grip, twist, push, and pull wire. They are used to cut and bend light gauge metal and pull staples and nails. The angular edges on the outside edge of the jaws are designed to ream down ridges and burs on the inside lip of cut pipes.

Most lineman’s pliers appear almost identical to one another, but some have extra tool functions like crimpers.

30. Give tips on how to maintain the functionality of pliers​


Answer:

Here are some tips for using pliers correctly:

1. When cutting wires or other objects, such as nails and rivets, always cut at right angles. Rocking the pliers or bending wire back and forth can damage the pliers’ sharpness and cutting ability.

2. Don’t use pliers that have worn-down or dull blades. These will require much more force to cut, which can injure your hands and damage the pliers further.

3. Pull on the handles of the pliers, don’t push them down. Pushing can damage the blade and can result in injury if the pliers slip.

4. Be sure to use pliers appropriate to the task. Using light pliers for heavy materials will damage the blade and can result in injury.

5. Don’t use pliers as a hammer – most pliers will fracture or break if used this way. Similarly, don’t hit the jaws with a hammer to increase their force, or attempt to extend the handles with other materials. Simply use larger pliers.

6. Avoid exposing pliers (and most other tools) to excessive heat that can damage the handle grip and integrity of the metal.

Remember that new tools often require “breaking in.” When you buy new pliers, they’ll likely be somewhat stiff and awkward to manipulate. This isn’t a flaw, but a sign of their newness.

You’ll need to oil them and manipulate them to ensure that they work to your satisfaction. Everyone has a slightly different style – some people prefer more “give” in their tools than others. You’ll learn what you like by using them over time and making small adjustments after every use.

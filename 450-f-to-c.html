4. At what temperature in a dry, dark place should potatoes and onions be stored?a. 50 – 650 F b. 40 – 450 F c. 60 – 850 F d. 70 – 950 F


Answer:

The answer is a. 50 – 65° F. As I’m assuming that the 650 above is actually 65 degrees, letter A is the correct answer. When you store potatoes in places that are cooler than 45 degrees, you risk discoloration.  Onions have to be stored in a cool, dark place to avoid moisture and light.

Explanation:

Storing Potatoes Not very cool Humid Dark

Potatoes should not be kept in the refrigerator. When stored in a very cool place, the potato’s starch breaks down into sugar and the potato starts to darken. This is why a cellar is a good place for storing potatoes, which can tolerate up to 90% humidity.  

When exposed to light, potatoes can accumulate solanine, an alkaloid that can be toxic at higher levels, so it’s best to store your potatoes in the dark.

Storing Onions Well-ventilated Less humid Dry

Onions need air to move around them, so a well-ventilated storage place such as a counter-top or a basket will do.  

Onions require less humidity than potatoes, around 70%. Yellow onions last longer, while white and sweet onions are more perishable. To preserve them better, you can keep them in the refrigerator, but cover them with paper towels to keep them dry.

Question:

Convert the following from celsius to fahrenheit and vice versa.

Explanation:

To convert from Fahrenheit to Celsius:

C° = (F° - 32)/9 x 5

Begin by subtracting 32 from the Fahrenheit number.

Divide the answer by 9.

Then multiply that answer by 5.

To convert from Celsius to Fahrenheit:

F° = (C° x 9/5) +32

Begin by multiplying the Celsius temperature by 9.

Divide the answer by 5.

Now add 32.

F= 9/5C+32 , so C=5/9(F-32)

Answer

E. 32⁰F

14. 2. A compound containing sodium, chlorine, and oxygen is 25.42% sodium by mass. A 3.25 gsample gives 4.33 x 1022 atoms of oxygen. What is the empirical formula?​


Answer: NaCIO2

Explanation:

Step 1

Percent oxygen in the sample:

4.33 x 1022 atoms divided by 6.022 x 1023 atoms/mol = 0.071903 mol

0.071903 mol times 16.00 g/mol = 1.15045 g

1.15045 g/3.25 g = 0.3540 = 35.40%

Step 2

Percent chlorine:

100 minus (25.42 + 35.40) = 39.18%

Step 3

Assume 100 g of the compound is present. This converts percents to grams. Determine moles:

Na = 25.42 g/23.0 g/mol = 1.105

CI= 39.18 g/35.453 g/mol = 1.105

0=35.40 g/ 16.00 g/mol = 2.2125

Step 4

And, finish with lowest whole-number ratio:

Divide by 1.105 to get lowest whole-number ratio of 1: 1:2

Therefore the empirical formula = NaClO2

18. Use the atomic mass of the elements to determine the percent of composition by mass of sodium and chlorine in table salt (sodium chloride).Sodium=Na=23.0 gChlorine=Cl=35.5 gSodium Chloride=NaCl=58.5 g​


Solution:

[tex]\text{mass percent of element} = \frac{\text{atomic mass × subscript}}{\text{molar mass of compound}} × 100[/tex]

For Na

[tex]\text{mass percent of Na} = \frac{\text{atomic mass Na × subscript}}{\text{molar mass of NaCl}} × 100[/tex]

[tex]\text{mass percent of Na} = \frac{\text{23.0 g × 1}}{\text{58.5 g}} × 100[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{mass percent of Na = 39.32\%}}[/tex]

For Cl

[tex]\text{mass percent of Cl} = \frac{\text{atomic mass Cl × subscript}}{\text{molar mass of NaCl}} × 100[/tex]

[tex]\text{mass percent of Cl} = \frac{\text{35.5 g × 1}}{\text{58.5 g}} × 100[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{mass percent of Cl = 60.68\%}}[/tex]

29. 2.) If Sodium (Na) has a mass of 3.33 g,how many moles does Sodium (Na) have? Atomic mass: 23 gMass given: 3.33 g No. of moles: 1Atomic mass: 23 g​


Answer:

To find the number of moles of a substance, you can use the formula:

moles = mass (g) / molar mass (g/mol)

In this case, we know that the mass of sodium is 3.33 g and its molar mass (or atomic mass) is 23 g/mol. So, plugging these values into the formula:

moles = 3.33 g / 23 g/mol = 0.14 mol

So, sodium (Na) has 0.14 moles.

Answer:

50 kg · 9.81 m/s² = 492 kg·m/s²

1 kg·m/s² is the same as 1 Newton. A Newton is a unit of force. So is a pound. And 492 N is approximately 111 lbs.

14. object A is 100 kg and object B is 50 kg. what does this mean


#BrainlyFast

If this is the only given statement of the problem and you did not forgot to add some valuable information in your question, then it simply mean that object B has a lesser mass compared to Object A. Object A has twice as much mass compared to object B.

It also means that Object A has greater inertia compared to object B.

Inertia is about the resistance of an object to change its motion. Force is the one that can change motion. Inertia is directly proportional to its mass. The bigger the mass the higher the inertia and the lesser the mass the lesser the inertia.

Law of inertia states that:

The object in motion will continue in motion unless acted upon by a net force.The object at rest will remain at rest unless acted upon by a net force.

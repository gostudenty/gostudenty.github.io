8. imagine life with and without electromagnetic waves​


Answer:

life without electromagnetic waves

No transfer of energy from the sun to the Earth, so no life. If there are no EM waves in the world, then, there would be nothing. ... Look up the electromagnetic spectrum to see examples of electromganetic waves. Radio, Microwave, Infrared, Visible light, Ultraviolet, X-rays, Gamma rays.

life with electromagnetic waves

Everyday life is pervaded by artificially made electromagnetic radiation: food is heated in microwave ovens, airplanes are guided by radar waves, television sets receive electromagnetic waves transmitted by broadcasting stations, and infrared waves from heaters provide warmth.

Answer:

It would be cold and colorless (and there probably would be NO life). The sun's energy is electromagnetic waves. The light we see is electromagnetic waves.

The electromagnetic spectrum is huge. What the human eye can see is only a tiny tiny slice of what is there.

There wouldn't be any Universe as we know it. Electromagnetic waves is what keeps the electron tied to the atom. If the electron did not communicate with protons in the nucleus of the atom, nothing of any physical substance could exist.

Light, for example, is both electromagnetic wave and particle as is the whole electromagnetic spectrum. Electromagnetic waves travel at 3.0 x 10^8 m / sec (186,262 miles / second).

A Universe filled with matter needs electromagnetic waves just to exist.

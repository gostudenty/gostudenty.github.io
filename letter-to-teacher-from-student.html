3. A teacher writes the following word on the board. A R I T H M E T I C He tells his students to randomly pick one letter from the word. What is the probability that Jonah will pick the letter T?


The probability for an event to happen, P(x), can be computed by P(x) = number of favorable outcomes/number of possible outcomes.

That being said, in this probllem our number of favorable outcomes is 2, having 2 T's in the problem.  And the number of possible outcomes is 10, having 10 letters in the word arithmetic.

Thus, P(x) = 2/10 = 1/5 = 0.2 = 20%

If I were the student in this situation, I would understand the school's rule and try to obtain an admission slip as soon as possible. If I was unable to obtain one because the personnel in-charge was not there, I would politely explain the situation to the teacher and ask if I could still take the quiz. I would also offer to bring in the admission slip as soon as it is available, to show that I am taking responsibility for my absence.

11. (a) How many moles of O2 are needed to burn 1.50 moles of C8H18? (b) How many grams of O2 are needed to burn 10.0 g of C8H18?


(a) How many moles of O₂ are needed to burn 1.50 moles of C₈H₁₈? Solution:

Step 1: Write the balanced chemical equation.

2C₈H₁₈ + 25O₂ → 16CO₂ + 18H₂O

Step 2: Determine the mole ratio needed.

mole ratio = 2 mol C₈H₁₈ : 25 mol O₂

Step 3: Calculate the number of moles of O₂ needed using the mole ratio.

[tex]\text{moles of O₂ = 1.50 mol C₈H₁₈} × \frac{\text{25 mol O₂}}{\text{2 mol C₈H₁₈}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{moles of O₂ = 18.75 mol}}[/tex]

(b) How many grams of O₂ are needed to burn 10.0 g of C₈H₁₈?Solution:

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of C₈H₁₈ and O₂.

For C₈H₁₈

molar mass = (12.01 g/mol × 8) + (1.008 g/mol × 18)

molar mass = 114.224 g/mol

For O₂

molar mass = 16.00 g/mol × 2

molar mass = 32.00 g/mol

Step 2: Determine the mole ratio needed.

mole ratio = 2 mol C₈H₁₈ : 25 mol O₂

Step 3: Calculate the mass of O₂ needed using the mole ratio.

[tex]\text{mass of O₂ = 10.0 g C₈H₁₈} × \frac{\text{1 mol C₈H₁₈}}{\text{114.224 g C₈H₁₈}} × \frac{\text{25 mol O₂}}{\text{2 mol C₈H₁₈}} × \frac{\text{32.00 g O₂}}{\text{1 mol O₂}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{mass of O₂ = 35.02 g}}[/tex]

22. Balance the following chemical equation, then answer the following question. C8H18(g)+O2(g)→CO2(g)+H2O(g) How many grams of oxygen are required to react with 16.0 grams of octane (C8H18) in the combustion of octane in gasoline?


Balancing the equation:

2C₆H₁₈ + 21O₂ -> 12CO₂ + 18H₂O

Note: Balance the Cs and Hs first, since they only appear at one substance in both sides.

Stoichiometry:

Take note of the molar masses of octane and oxygen gas.

C₆H₁₈ - 114.23 g/mol

O₂ - 32.00 g/mol

First, find the number of moles of octane in the given mass above.

16.0 g × [1 / 114.23 g/mol] = 0.140 mol C₆H₁₈

Now, use the mole ratio of octane to oxygen to obtain the number of moles of oxygen involved in the reaction.

N mol O₂ / 0.140 mol C₆H₁₈ = 21 mol O₂ / 2 mol C₆H₁₈

N / 0.140 = 21 / 2

N = 1.47 [mol O₂]

Finally, find the number of grams in 1.47 moles of oxygen gas.

1.47 mol × 32.00 g/mol = 47.04 g O₂

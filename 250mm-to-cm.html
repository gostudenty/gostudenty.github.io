10. The wheel of an automobile revolves at 700rpm. How fast does it move in kilometer per hour if the radius of its wheel is 250mm?


Hello!

The wheel of an automobile revolves at 700 Rpm. How fast does it move in kilometer per hour if the radius of its wheel is 250 mm ?

We have the following data:

V (linear velocity) = ? (in km/h)  

f (frequency) = 700 Rpm  

R (radius) =250 mm → 0.25 m  

Adopt: π ≈ 3.14  

if: 1min = 60s

We are going to transform the frequency of rotations per minute (Rpm) in revolutions per second (Hz), let's see:

[tex]f = \dfrac{Rpm}{60}[/tex]

[tex]f = \dfrac{700}{60}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{f \approx 11.66\:Hz}[/tex]

If the Period (T) is inversely proportional to the Frequency, then, the Period (T), will be:

[tex]T = \dfrac{1}{f}[/tex]

[tex]T = \dfrac{1}{11.66}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{T \approx 0.085\:s}[/tex]

Now, let's find the linear velocity, applying the data to the formula, let's see:

[tex]V = \dfrac{2*\pi *R}{T}[/tex]

[tex]V = \dfrac{2*3.14*0.25}{0.085}[/tex]

[tex]V = \dfrac{1.57}{0.085}[/tex]

[tex]V \approx 18.47\:m/s[/tex]

Now, we must convert m/s to km/h, let's see:

[tex]18.47\:\dfrac{m}{s} = 18.47\:\dfrac{3600\:s}{1000\:m} = \dfrac{66492}{1000} = 66.492 \to \boxed{\boxed{\approx 66.5\:km/h}}\end{array}}\qquad\checkmark[/tex]

or as you prefer, multiply by 3.6  

[tex]18.47\:m/s*3.6\:\to\boxed{\boxed{\approx 66.5\:km/h}}\end{array}}\qquad\checkmark[/tex]

Answer:

The linear speed of the car wheel is approximately 66.5 km/h

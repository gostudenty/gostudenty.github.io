Ozone Dangers

Photocopiers that make use of toner ink emit high levels of ozone during electrical discharges. Regular exposure to ozone is synonymous with a multitude of health problems, including headaches, nausea and dermatitis, as well as severe irritation in the throat, lungs and eyes.

Substances, events, or circumstances can be considered risks if their nature allows them to do harm to health, life, property, or any other valuable interest, even if only hypothetically. What is Physical Hazard?

A physical hazard is a substance, factor, or condition that has the potential to hurt people when they come into touch with it. They are divided into two categories: occupational hazards and environmental hazards. Ergonomic risks, radiation, heat and cold stress, vibration risks, and noise risks are only a few examples of physical risks. To reduce physical risks, engineering controls are frequently applied.

What is Chemical Hazard?

A chemical hazard that poses a risk to life or health.

Other types of hazards

Ergonomic hazards - Physical conditions that put the musculoskeletal system, such as the lower back muscles or ligaments, the tendons or nerves of the hands/wrists, or the bones surrounding the knees, at danger of harm, resulting in a musculoskeletal disease, are referred to as ergonomic hazards. Examples include poor equipment design, workstation design, (postural) or workflow design, manual handling, repetitive movement, and so forth.Biological hazards are biological chemicals that are harmful to the health of living creatures, most notably humans. Microorganisms that cause disease, viruses, poisons (from biological sources), spores, fungus, and bioactive compounds are among them.Psychological hazard: Any occupational danger that impacts workers' psychological and physical well-being, including their capacity to participate in a work setting with other people, is referred to as a psychological hazard.An environmental hazard is a material, a situation, or an event that has the potential to endanger the surrounding natural environment / or harm people's health, such as pollution and natural catastrophes like storms and earthquakes.

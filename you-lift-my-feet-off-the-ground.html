10. A 35 kg chair is lifted 5 m off ground. What is its potential energy


Good Day....

Question:

A 35 kg chair is lifted 5 m off ground. What is its potential energy

Gravitational Potential Energy is related to mass, gravity, and height.

The formula is P.E. = mass × gravity × height

Given:

Mass = 35 kg

gravity = constant (9.8 m/s^2)

Height = 5 meters

P.E. (potential energy) = ? unknown

Solution:

P.E. = m × g × h

      = 35 kg × 9.8 m/s^2 × 5 m

      = 1, 715 kg . m^2/s^2

       = 1,715 J

Answer :

potential energy = 1,715 J

13. Select the correct answer. Mason lifts a box off the ground and places it in a car that is 0.50 meters high. If he applies a force of 8.0 newtons to lift this suitcase, what is the work done?


Answer:

The work done is 4J.

Explanation:

In this problem, we are to solve the work done by lifting up a box and placed it inside a car to a certain distance. To do this, we will be using the formula

W = F · d

where

W     is the work, the unit is in N·m or Joules (J)

F      is the applied force, the unit is in Newton (N)

d      is the distance, the unit is in meter (m)

Given information

d = 0.50 m

F = 8.0 N

W = ?

Solving the problem

Let us solve for the work done by the mason on the box. Let us use the given formula for work and substitute the given data.

W = F · d

W = (8 N)().50 m)

W = 4 N·m or 4 J

Therefore, the work done by the mason on the box is 4 J.

26. A 35.6 kg chair is lifted 5.5 m off the ground. What is its potential energy?


PROBLEM:

A 35.6 kg chair is lifted 5.5 m off the ground. What is its potential energy?

SOLUTION:POTENTIAL ENERGYit is the latent energy in an object at rest; stored energygravitational potential energy - energy stored in an object due to its position above the Earth's surface [tex]\sf \underline{\boxed{\rm PE= mgh}} [/tex]; in which PE is the potential energy, m is for the mass, g for the gravity (which is constant, 9.8 m/s²) while h is for the height.

[tex]\rm PE= mgh[/tex]

[tex]\rm PE= 35.6\:kg × 9.8\: \frac{m}{s²} × 5.5 \: m[/tex]

[tex]\rm PE= 348.88 \:\frac{kgm}{s²} × 5.5 \: m[/tex]

[tex]\rm PE= 1918.84 \:\frac{kgm {}^{2} }{s²} [/tex]

One unit of kgm²/s² is equivalent of 1 Joule (J).

ANSWER:

The potential energy of the chair is [tex]\underline{\boxed{\rm 1918.84\:J}}[/tex].

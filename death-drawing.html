13. What similarities between Everyman and humankind can you draw out from the conversation of Everyman and Death?Provide evidences for the comparisons.Sana may correct answer​


Explanation:

Everyman is a morality play, which details the life and death of the allegorical Everyman, who embodies all of humanity.

Death warns Everyman that he will be judged by God when he dies.

Terrified, Everyman turns to Fellowship, but his friends desert him. Everyman then hopes that his Goods will comfort him, but his possessions also abandon him.

Everyman then turns to his Good Deeds, but they have been weakened by his sins.

Everyman eventually arrives in Heaven with the help of his other attributes, but he learns that only his Good Deeds will come with him. He'll be judged by his actions alone.

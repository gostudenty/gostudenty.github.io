23. What is the molality of a solution containing 100 grams of glucose (C6H12O6, molar mass = 180 g/mol) dissolved in 2.5 kg of water?


Solution:

Note: Glucose is the solute and water is the solvent.

Step 1: Calculate the number of moles of solute.

[tex]\text{moles of solute} = \frac{\text{mass of solute}}{\text{molar mass of solute}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of solute} = \frac{\text{100 g}}{\text{180 g/mol}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of solute = 0.55556 mol}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the molality of solution.

[tex]\text{molality} = \frac{\text{moles of solute}}{\text{mass of solvent (kg)}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{molality} = \frac{\text{0.55556 mol}}{\text{2.5 kg}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{molality} = 0.22 \: m}[/tex]

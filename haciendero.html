17. 10. Who do higantes portray during the Spanish rule and why did locals use them? A, hacienderos, to mock the bad hacienderos B. farmers, to show the hard work of the farmersC. Spaniards, to show people's high respect to the Spaniards D. Filipinos, to show that Filipinos are the real giants in the country​


Answer:

C. Spaniards, to show people's high respect to the Spaniards

Explanation:

Higantes Festival is a secular celebration initiated by the Municipality of Angono to express gratitude to its patron Saint Clement, in which the parade of giants is held on Sunday before the town feast on November 23.

Base on Angono folklore, the ‘higante’ was used as symbol of agrarian protest during the waning years of the Spanish colonization when Angono was a hacienda (large tract of lands).

21. A festival in Rizal province as a form of silent protest againts the rich Spanish hacienderos?​


Answer:

Higantes Festival

Explanation:

The Higantes Festival is a local festival held annually In Angono, Rizal, Philippines, where hundreds of giant papier-mâché puppets are paraded, representing the common people's mockery of the bad hacienda (land) owners of the past during Spanish colonial rule.

It has evolved into also celebrating the feast of Pope St. Clement I, every 22 and 23 November.[1]

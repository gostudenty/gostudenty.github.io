3. Ana, a member of JC Company procurement team, is tasked to procure bond papers for the company's use. She knew White Company, who previously assured her that they sell bond papers at the cheapest price in the area, so she went straight to them to purchase the needed items. Later on, she learned that Dino Company also sells bond papers but at a price much cheaper than White Company's. Ana went back to White Company and threatened them that she will have their contract annulled for being voidable on the ground that they defrauded her when the company represented that it sells bond papers at the cheapest price in the area. Is Ana correct? Why?


Answer

Ana is wrong because she will be violating the contract between JC Company and White Company. And second, she is at fault for making rushed decisions for the company. Ana should have made a procurement list before entering a contract with White Company.

Explanation

The contract between JC Company, represented by Ana, and White Company is called a Supplier Contract or Supply Contract. A Supplier Contract is defined as an agreement wherein the seller promises to supply goods or services that the buyer needs IN A CERTAIN TIME DURATION at a price agreed upon by the buyer. Hence, Ana cannot nullify the contract because there has been a legal agreement.
